EVENTS @ LSA

Date: 22nd November 2012

Event: 17th Founder’s Day Celebration

Venue: Kalamandir

Highlight: Lakshmipat Singhania Academy Celebrated its 17th Founder’s Day. The academy celebrates the birth anniversary of its Founding father Late Shri Lakshmipat Singhania on 23rd November which culminates into formal Founder’s Day programme. Shrimati Sharda Singhania, President, Executive Committee of Lakshmipat Singhania Academy presided over the function. Dr. Subir Chowdhury Ex. Directior of of IIM Joka & member of managing committee of Lakshmipat Singhania Academy was the Guest of Honour.

The programme began with a welcome song followed by the welcome address delivered jointly by the Head Girl, Soumabha Bandopadhyay and the Head Boy, Aman Jain. After the lighting of the lamp and garlanding of the bust of Lala Lakshmipat Singhania, the Principal Mrs. Meena Kak delivered the keynote address followed by the reading of the Annual Report, elucidating the achievements of the academy and its students for excelling in all spheres. . Shrimati Sharda Singhania & Dr. Subir Chowdhury also addressed the gathering.

This was followed by the Annual Award ceremony where students were felicitated for not only their academic and co-curricular achievements, but also for their exemplary conduct in school. First, the Class X and XII CBSE Board toppers (session 2011-2012) were felicitated. This was followed by special category of prizes comprising the Principal’s Award and the Prashasti Samman.

The cultural programme was a dance drama named-“Samay Sopan-down the ages”. It portrayed the incredible journey of our motherland from the Vedic age to the modern era of man-machines. The presentation personified motherland India as ‘’Bharta mata’’, who narrates her story to her children. The tradition and culture of India was well displayed through the classical dance forms of Kathak, Bhartanatyam, Mohiniattyam and others, through the music of a particular era, like Carnatic for the Dravidian era. The essence of belonging to the 21st century found its presence through the various video clippings that preceded every scene to give it a “real” and lively look, almost theatrical in its appeal. However, a thought provoking question of whether India is truly independent even after six decades of independence was left lurking in our minds at the end of the entire rendition. The performers very explicitly pointed at the social evils that ail our country- corruption, poverty, illiteracy and unemployment to make us aware of where our country is heading to. However the positive note could never be missed highlighting the fact that the youth of India is still vibrant and socially aware. With their efforts and hard work they will definitely be able to curb all such evils and a time will come when India will emerge as a socially developed country and will lead the exemplary path to success. Around 200 students from classes 6 to 12 presented this beautifully choreographed, dramatized and enacted item that was scripted in-house. The audience was left spell bound and enthralled. 

      

Date: 18th October 2012

Event: Pre Puja Celebraton

Highlight: We, at Lakshmipat Singhania Academy, took this opportunity to educate the students about the true essence of celebrating Durga Puja. The day’s assembly comprised a movie which threw light not only on the festivities associated with Durga Puja but also how the post-puja activities can prove to be detrimental for the environment.We, considered it an appropriate time to share our happiness with our grandparents at the Help Age India Home at Chetla. So we herald in this festive season by instilling this feeling of love, concern and respect for those around us.

Date: 24th August 2012
Event: exeBIT- an Inter School IT Fest
Participants: Classes IX- XII students of 23 schools including Lakshmipat Singhania Academy.
Highlight: The Chief Guest for the opening ceremony was Mr. Shubhashish Addy, Chairman and MD of ACES group of Companies. 23 schools participated.The list of events included Photoshop, C++ programming, PowerPoint presentation, Website-designing, Counter strike, Installation art, Quiz, Photography and Minesweeper. Workshops on Android programming and Paint was also organised.The overall trophy was won by Lakshmipat Singhania Academy, whereas St. Xavier’s Collegiate School were declared the 1st Runners Up and Sushila Birla Girls’ School were the 2nd Runners Up.

Date: 15th August 2012

Event: Independence Day Celebration (All Faith Prayer Meeting)
Venue: 8th Floor Assembly Hall
Participants: Classes III – XII. All cubs, scouts, bulbuls and guides from our school and 5 other schools of Kolkata.
Teachers In charge: Mr. D.S.Gill, Ms. M.Mukhopadhyay, Mr. S. Chakraborty, Mr. K.Paul, Mr. J. Chakraborty, Ms. A. Sahoo, Ms. A. Nahid, Mr. S.Nayak
Highlights: The national flag and the Bharat Scouts and Guides flag were hoisted in the ground floor assembly hall followed by All Faith Prayer meeting in the 8th floor assembly hall. The scouts and guides of our school as well as of the other schools participated in it. Prayers of all religions were said and a few devotional songs were sung. This prayer meeting reinforced secularism and discipline among our children.

                                

Date: 3rd July 2012

Event: Badge Pinning Ceremony of Integrity Club

Teachers’ In Charge: Ms. Anita Chandra and Ms. Jayati Goswami

Highlight: A pilot mission initiative to re kindle values in School children by Lakshmipat Singhania Academy. This club has been conceived with a mission that the values in the society will be re kindled by the Young Champions of Ethics. The YCEs’ will propagate ‘ human values’ in their families, neighbourhood, school community and society at large to strengthen value based culture in the country.

The YCEs will hold the responsibilities of carrying their club mission and vision and utilizing them in real day-to-day life.
