Workshops for Teachers (Session 2012-2013)

 
Date: 1st October 2012
Event: Teacher’s Workshop on the ‘Art of Story Telling’
Organised By: British Council in association with Oxford Bookstore
Venue: iLead
Participants: Ms. Teresa Gomes & Ms. Ujjaini Roy
Resource Person: Xanthe Gresham
Highlight: The workshop was on the art of story telling conducted by Xanthe Gresham, a globally acclaimed story teller. It was focused on the use of propos, music, action and involvement of the participants.

 


Date: 22nd September 2012
Event: Teachers Workshop on CCE Curriculum
Organised By: Bharatiya Bhasha Parisad
Attended By: Ms. P.Gupta, Ms. S.R.Dey, Ms. P.Dayani, Ms. R.Shaw and Mr. S. Chakraborty
Highlight: CCE curriculum and grading pattern for classes VI – X was discussed thoroughly.

 

 Date: 21st September 2012
Event: Workshop on Cyber Security
Organised By: Kolkata Police
Venue: Lal bazaar police station
Attended by: Mr. N. Motilal
Highlight: The workshop will be useful for parents as well as students as it will benefit them to protect themselves from cyber stalking and cyber bullying. Real life scenarios were discussed which were eye openers.


 
 Date: 7th September 2012
Event: Workshop on Learning Disability
Resource Person: Mr. Arun Kapur
Venue: Manochetna Manovikas Kendra
Participant: Ms. Anju Munshi
Highlight: The resource person spoke about dyslexia and stress in academics.

 

Date: 1st September 2012
Event: Workshop for teachers on Money Matters
Organised By: ICICI Bank
Highlight: The latest facilities available in ICICI bank like P.F. account, mobile applications etc. were discussed.

 

Date: 31st August, 2012
Event: Workshop on language teaching organized by British Council
Teachers who attended the workshop: Ms. Ushita Chatterjee and Ms. Anurupta Sodhi and Ms. Arlene Guhathakurta
Venue: British Council
Resource person: Ms. Arpita Banerjee
Highlights: The teachers were given exposure to creative and communicative ways of teaching the English Language
 


Date: 21st August, 2012
Event: Workshop on Phonics
Name of the workshop: Teacher’s Development Programme on Phonics organized by Cambridge University Press
Resource person: Ms. Imke Herold
Teacher who attended the workshop: Kaveri Singh
Highlights: The workshop promoted ‘Jolly phonics’ which believed in teaching of sounds and not letters. There are 44 sounds in the English Language. According to ‘Jolly phonics’, each sound will have an action and a story related to the action.

 

Date: 30th July 2012
Event: Workshop on Science and Mathematics
Organized by Orient Blackswan Pvt. Ltd.
Venue: St’ Thomas Church
Resource person:  Dr. Champaknath Attavar
Participants: Ms. Sharmita Bhattacharya, Ms. Sushmita Roy
Highlights: Simple activities were conducted to explain addition and subtraction, H.C.F and L.C.M etc. whereas in science simple experiments were shown like creating a cloud in a bottle, models of radicals and molecular formula and how to fool our senses.

 
Date: 27th - 29th July 2012
Event: Teacher’s workshop on First Aid Refresher Course for Scouts
Organised By: Ganganagar Scouts Group- Barasat
Venue: Ganganagar, Barasat
Participants: Mr. Kallol Paul
Highlight: All the information regarding   first aid was given very elaborately.
 


Date: 23rd July 2012
Event: Workshop on Practicing and Presenting Language
Resource person: Ms. Mini Joseph
Participants: Ms. Anurupta Sodhi and Ms. Arlene GuhaThakurta
Highlights: Learnt the methodology of teaching grammar, especially the second conditional. It was emphasized that the traditional way of teaching grammar must be synthesized with the play-way method which will encourage the students to grasp the rules and laws of grammar.

Date: 7th July, 2012

Venue: 8th Floor AV Room

Date: 23rd June 2012
Event : Teachers workshop on types of learning disability
Resource Person: Ms. Kate Currawalla (President of MDA), Ms. Mindy Eichhorn
Participants: Ms. Madhu Berry, Ms. Aloka Sen Sharma, Ms. Teresa Gomes
Highlight: The workshop was to spread awareness among teachers and parents to identify the early symptoms of Dyslexia in a child and refer him / her to an educational clinic at a Family Welfare Centre or hospital to a qualified private practitioner for a complete evaluation.
 


Date: 22nd June 2012

Date: 22nd June 2012
Event : Teachers workshop on Breaking Through Dyslexia
Venue: Jyothirmai Club, Garcha Road
Participants: Ms. Pronita Gupta, Ms. Nandini Chanda, Ms. Anita Chandra

Date: 15th  June 2012
Event: Teacher’s Workshop on Art of Negotiation
Organised By:  British Council, India
Participants: Mr. Darshan S. Gill
Highlight: Defined negotiation and identified the various types of negotiation. Learnt how to make each negotiation very effective.
 


Date: 14th June 2012
Event: Workshop on HR Skills for Non HR People
Resource Person: Mr. Andrew Steele
Venue: Hotel Hindustan
Participant: Ms. Krishna Nandy
Highlight:
The workshop began with the introduction of members who were mostly from different companies.
The workshop dealt with factors which were essential for Appraisal & Recruitment.  The first skill which was taught was listening skills. Next the Performance Appraisal Process was taken up.
The 3rd point to be discussed was effective feedback. Finally, the types of questions to be asked during recruitment were discussed. Altogether, it was a good learning experience & enjoyable.

 

 Date: 13th June 2012
Event: Workshop on Importance and tools of building effective relationships
Venue: Hotel Hindusthan International
Resource person: Mr. Andrew Steel
Name of the participant: Ms. Anjali Chopra
Highlight: It helped to realize the importance of building an effective relationship to have a motivated and a willing team. It also dealt with the influencing skills, some time wasters at workplace and self management.
